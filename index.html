Ji-Sung Park

Park Ji-Sung; born 25 February 1981 is a South Korean former professional footballer who played as a midfielder. He currently serves as club ambassador for Manchester United, for whom he played from 2005 to 2012. Born in Seoul, Park is the most successful Asian player in history, having won 19 trophies in his career. He is the first Asian footballer to have won the UEFA Champions League, to play in a UEFA Champions League final, as well as the first Asian to have won the FIFA Club World Cup. Park was able to play anywhere across the midfield and was noted for his exceptional fitness level, discipline, work ethic and off-the-ball movement. His remarkable endurance levels and pace earned him the nickname "Three-Lungs" Park.

  • 1981 - Born in Seoul, South Korea
  • 1999 - Joins Myongji University and plays for their college football team
  • April, 2000 - Makes his senior international team debut for South Korea in a regional qualifier for the 2000 Summer Olympics against Laos, at the age of 19
  • June, 2000 - Signs with Kyoto-based J1 League side Kyoto Purple Sanga, two years into his studies
  • 2002 - Scores a memorable match-winning goal against Portugal in the 2002 FIFA World Cup, which advanced South Korea into the knockout stages of the tournament for the first time
  • 2003 - Joins Dutch club PSV Eiindhove, managed by former national team coach Guus Hiddink
  • 2005 - Scores his first UEFA Champions league goal in a semi-final match againist AC Milan
  • July, 2005 - Signs for English club Manchester United, becoming the first East Asian player to do so
  • October, 2005 - Becomes the first Asian player ever to captain Manchester United, after replacing Ryan Giggs during a Champions League home group match against Lille
  • December, 2005 - Scores his first goal for Manchester United, during a 3-1 win over Birmingham City in the League Cup fifth round
  • 2008 - Becomes the first Asian player to win the UEFA Champions League
  • 2009 - Is the first Asian player in history to play in a UEFA Champions League final
  • 2010 - Captains South Korea at the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa
  • April, 2012 - Makes his 205th and final appearance for Manchester United in a 1-0 derby defeat against Manchester City
  • July, 2012 - Signs for newly promoted Premier League side Queens Park Rangers, after 7 years at Manchester United
  • 2014 - Retires at his former club PSV Eindhoven
  • October, 2014 - Becomes a Manchester United club ambassador
